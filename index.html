OUR MISSION

Create opportunities for high school seniors to gain any exposure in their desired profession through mentorship and career-based training which DeKalb County professionals

OUR VISION

A career preparation program that we develop future industry leaders from DeKalb County high schools.

Project Learning Is For Everyone (Project LIFE) is a non-profit organization whose primary initiative is to support a career-networking corridor between DeKalb County professionals and high school seniors. Crafted by founders, India Ali, Omari Crawford,and Jason Little—proud DeKalb County School System graduates, Project Life was established to help support the enhancement of career interests of high school seniors in DeKalb County Schools through career-focused training and mentorship. DeKalb County has an esteemed history of graduating talented and equipped professionals who continue to add value in competitive industries worldwide.

Ali, Crawford, and Little believe the students of DeKalb County are fully capable of emerging as global industry leaders; however, as high school seniors approach the next chapter of their lives, career exposure is pivotal. Project LIFE aims to assist DeKalb County high school seniors achieve their career goals by surrounding them with a broader network of supporters.

At its fundamental principle, Project LIFE focuses on offering volunteer service to help high school seniors as we aid them with gaining a foundational understanding of their desired professions. Project LIFE will work with designated points of contact within DeKalb County Schools to manage protocols, establish and maintain structured student-to-professional dialogue for career information. As authorized, the team at Project LIFE will create opportunities for students to gain early career-driven experiences by pairing them with DeKalb County professionals who will offer structured, career-based supplemental instruction through ongoing mentorship and training.

Project LIFE will offer annual scholarships to a select group of students through an internal competitive application process. At the beginning of the spring semester, Project LIFE will release an application package to high school seniors enrolled in DeKalb County schools. DeKalb County seniors—who participated in extra—curricular activities during their high school careers— are eligible for Project LIFE's competitive scholarship. Once selected, students will receive a monetary scholarship and the opportunity to pair with an experienced DeKalb County professional of their specific career interest. This collaborative pairing supports Project LIFE's aim to foster meaningful relationships between scholarship recipients and DeKalb County professionals.

Project LIFE wholeheartedly believes that every student can succeed. We are committed to helping students achieve their career goals by extending our support to help build and expand their pool of resources. Recognizing the tremendous number of accomplished students who hail from The DeKalb County School System, it is evident that students are equipped with the necessary tools to become life-long learners and productive leaders far beyond our local community. Project LIFE will passionately serve to assist students with achieving their future career goals.

Attorney India Y. Ali is a Georgia native, born and raised in Lithonia and Decatur. Ali is an entrepreneur - managing her own law firm and an investment company, as well as basketball coach, artist, and philanthropist. Attorney Ali is heavily involved in her beloved DeKalb County community, and has started volunteer outreach programs and currently offers donation-based legal seminars.

In addition to Project LIFE, Attorney Ali serves on the board of the South DeKalb Tribe and The Starr Institute. Since 2019, Attorney Ali has financially sponsored high school and amateur sports programs in Dekalb County, and currently is the Assistant Basketball Coach of the Lady Panthers at her alma mater, Southwest DeKalb High School, in Decatur, Georgia.

In 2008, Attorney Ali received her undergraduate degree in Business Marketing and Fine Arts at Hofstra University where she played NCAA Division I basketball. Attorney Ali received her Juris Doctorate and Master's in Business Administration from North Carolina Central University School of Law and School of Business in 2013.

Omari Crawford; a Decatur, GA native, is a proud product of the DeKalb County Public School System and a 2004 graduate of Southwest DeKalb High School. While attending Southwest DeKalb, Omari participated in various academic, athletic and community service extracurricular activities. Crawford—a former Georgia state track and field 800-meter champion—attended Florida A&M University (FAMU) on a track and field scholarship. At FAMU, Omari excelled as a student-athlete—earning a conference title and was a member of the all-academic conference team for three consecutive years.

Omari received a Bachelor's in Political Science and a Master's in Public Administration in 2008 and 2009 respectively. After FAMU, Omari earned his Juris Doctorate and Master's in Business Administration from North Carolina Central University School of Law and School of Business in 2013. Omari is licensed to practice law in Georgia and has litigation experience in personal injury, civil rights, and government.

Omari's passion for mentoring students can be attributed to his unique employment and volunteer experiences with the DeKalb County Public School System. Omari spent time in the classroom as a substitute teacher, served as a pre-commencement speaker at multiple DeKalb County High Schools, and mentors young DeKalb County student-athletes. He serves as a Board Member for Project LIFE and the South DeKalb Family YMCA.

Jason Little matriculated through the DeKalb County Public School System and graduated from Cedar Grove High School in 2004. While at Cedar Grove, Jason served as Student Body President and was honored with the distinction of Mr. Cedar Grove during his senior year. He was a prominent member of his high school swim team, earning individual DeKalb County Championships and US Swimming GA State Relay Titles.

Jason continued his swimming career at Florida A&M University (FAMU), where he received a Bachelor of Science in Industrial Engineering in 2008. In 2009, Jason earned a Master's in Engineering Management from Florida State University. After years as a process engineer and supply chain consultant, Jason currently serves as Partner with Genpact within their Consumer Goods and Retail Industry Vertical."

Jason's dedication to Project LIFE builds upon his years of service to the DeKalb County community. Jason has established a platform of volunteering which includes tutoring students with standardized test preparation, serving as a water safety instructor and swim coach, and volunteering to increase local voter registration. Additionally, Jason created the Little Brother's Scholarship which provides scholarships to high school students who attend Historically Black Colleges and Universities.
